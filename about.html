About


A common error in the intubation process is the misplacement of the endotracheal tube into the esophagus instead of the trachea. This problem occurs in nearly 155 of intubations as reported by EMSWorld.

The most effective way to verify proper intubation is the measurement of end-tidal carbon dioxide exiting the tube.

Current methods of testing carbon dioxide include a disposable chemical color-changing test which may cost an EMS organization $10-12 each use.

We develop a multi-use device that can save EMS organizations thousands of dollars each year.
